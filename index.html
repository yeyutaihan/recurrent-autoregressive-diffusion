Recurrent Autoregressive Diffusion:
Global Memory Meets Local Attention

Abstract

Diffusion models have demonstrated remarkable success in generating high-fidelity data across continuous domains, while autoregressive models excel at capturing long-range dependencies in sequential data. However, combining the strengths of both paradigms—particularly at scale— remains an open challenge. We present Recurrent Autoregressive Diffusion (RAD), a novel generative architecture that integrates a recurrent global memory mechanism with a local diffusion transformer. At each autoregressive step, a compact recurrent state summarizes all previously generated context, which is then used to condition a local diffusion process responsible for generating the next segment. This design decouples global coherence from local fidelity: the recurrent module maintains consistency across the entire sequence, while the diffusion module ensures high-quality local generation. We validate RAD on long-form image synthesis, audio generation, and video prediction benchmarks, where it consistently outperforms baselines in both sample quality and computational efficiency. Our results suggest that recurrent global memory and local attention are highly complementary, opening a new direction for scalable generative modeling.
